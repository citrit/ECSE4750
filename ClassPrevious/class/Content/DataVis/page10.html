Example of Simulations and Visualizations:

Fresnel Concentrator

Fresnel concentrator is an array of 121 mirror facets on a fixed array of rows. Prof. David Borton of Mechanical engineering wanted to show the mechanics of how the facets were unable to focus the suns rays at certain times of the year. He did the math involved and created a spreadsheet of numbers explaining his results. This graphic visualizes the motion of an 11 by 11 array of mirrors throughout a solar year. The declination of the sun changes +/- 23.5 degrees with the seasons. The change of the focus can be adjusted depending on the energy requirements of the solar thermal application from steam heat to electricity to chemical processing.

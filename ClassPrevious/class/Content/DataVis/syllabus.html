DESCRIPTION:

The course will cover topics associated with advanced computer graphics including Graphical Application Programmer Interfaces (APIs) such as OpenGL, hi performance graphics engines and visualization pipelines. There will be a section on cross platform development and issues involved with porting graphics software. The course will also explore algorithms for extracting visual information from data sets, such as marching cubes, used for determining iso-surfaces, contours, and cut planes. There will be a short overview of Object Oriented design and Object Oriented systems followed by a section on creating visualizations using packages such as IRISExplorer and object oriented software libraries such as VTK and Open Inventor. The course will conclude with a term project in which the student models a real world phenomenon using any of the applications covered in class. Term project required. Prereqs: 35.475 Computer Graphics, 66.436 Data Structures or equivalent, some familiarity with Java/C++.

FACILITIES:

Term Project:

There will be a term project due at the end of the semester. The project will require the use of one of the visualization tools discussed to create an animation of some physical phenomena. The animation will need to include certain aspects of visualization and animation, these criteria will be specified at a later time.

Homework:

There will be seven programming homework assignments spaced over the semester. They are due the following Tuesday at midnight, this means you have one week to complete the assignments. The assignments will cover the current topic listed above. There are also in class modules to augment the course notes. There is no collaboration on homework assignments, you may discuss general topics or methods of solving a problem in groups but the final homework submission needs to be your own work.

Policies:

Late submissions: In order to receive any grade on a project, you must submit something by the deadline. If your project is not completed by the deadline, you should submit what you have at that time. You will be able to submit additions/corrections after the deadline to improve your grade. But the best policy is to get the work completed by the deadline. There will be a 5% deduction from your grade for every day it is late.

Do your own work: I am a professional developer. I look at code all day and understand program flow and programming paradigms. I have a nack for remembering code I have written so please do not find one of my old solutions and change the variables and hand it in as your own. This will mean a zero for the assignment and possible reporting to the office of student affairs.

Collaboration on the Final Project: There will be a complete description of the project and how the grouping will work. Details to follow.

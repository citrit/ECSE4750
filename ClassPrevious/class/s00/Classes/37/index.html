Rendering Pipeline Ctd

Today we saw more details of the rendering pipeline and Foley pictures. Some notes:

  1. The initial object surface is often curved, either a set of parametric bicubic patches, or a quadric surface (conic section).

  2. It is often approximated as a set of small flat faces, called facets.

  3. A lighting equation calculates the color at each pixel. Its input is

    1. The unit normal vector to the surface at that point.

    2. A unit vector pointing towards the light source.

    3. A unit vector pointing towards the viewer.

  4. Different surfaces have different lighting equations. A flat (Lambertian) surface, like a carpet, reflects light out equally in all directions.

  5. A specular (mirror) surface reflects light out only in one direction, at the angle of reflection.

  6. Most surfaces reflect more light, but not all, at the angle of reflection.

  7. Since the lighting equation, and its inputs, especially the surface normal, are expensive to calculate, approximations are common.
